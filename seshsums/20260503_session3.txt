Key findings:
- 5-8 ppb reduction in methane during lamp-ON periods — small but in the right direction.
  Given 96 lamps on an actual ship at sea vs. 4 lamps in a lab, any observable delta is
  scientifically meaningful.
- Main noise source: other ships passing, creating large methane plumes that swamp the signal.
- Windspeed sensor is confounded: uses temperature differential, so lamp heat corrupts
  readings during ON phases. Cannot use as contamination filter.
- Upward methane drift across the night (~1.84 to 2.10 ppm) is real; within-pair and LMM
  corrections are the right approach.

Next steps discussed:
- Methane rate-of-change filter to flag and exclude ship-plume contamination windows.
- More cycles / longer experiments to build statistical power.
